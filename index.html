About Me

I am a Physics graduate from Pondicherry University with research experience in spectroscopy, materials characterization, and experimental data analysis. At BARC Mumbai, I worked on high-pressure Raman spectroscopy using diamond anvil cells, covering pressure calibration, multi-region spectral acquisition, and reference material characterization. At IIT Tirupati, I synthesized nickel–cobalt hydroxide electrodes and characterized them using XRD and electrochemical methods. At the Indian Institute of Astrophysics, I built a stellar spectroscopy data-reduction pipeline using PyRAF. I also co-authored a paper presented at the DAE Symposium on Nuclear Physics, where my MSc dissertation work on compound nucleus decay using the Dynamical Cluster-decay Model was contributed.

I am currently applying for PhD positions in experimental condensed matter physics, with a focus on spectroscopy-based characterization and structure–property relationships in functional materials.
